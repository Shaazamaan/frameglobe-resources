1. The Pencil Grip

Writing Grip vs. Overhand Grip
Drawing of hand holding pencil like a wand (loose) vs like a pen (tight)

Don't hold it like a pen! Use the 'Overhand Grip' (holding it like a wand) for long, smooth lines. Use the writing grip only for tiny details.

2. Line Quality

Ghosting Lines

Practice the stroke in the air 2-3 times before touching the paper. This builds muscle memory for confident lines.

Shoulder Movement

Lock your wrist. Move from your shoulder. Wrist = wobbly lines. Shoulder = architectural straight lines.

3. The 3 Magic Shapes

Sphere, Cube, Cylinder drawn simply

Everything in the world is made of these 3 shapes.
• Apple = Sphere
• Arm = Cylinder
• House = Cube
Stop drawing "objects." Start drawing shapes.

4. Shading Basics

The Terminator Line
Sphere showing light side vs shadow side divider

The line where light turns into shadow is the most important part of realism. Make this transition soft for round objects and hard for boxy objects.

5. Proportions (Face)

Simple circle with cross in middle

1. Start with a Circle.
2. Cut it in half vertically & horizontally.
3. Eyes go on the horizontal line.
4. Nose is halfway between eyes and chin.
5. Mouth is halfway between nose and chin.

6. Daily Warm-up

Do this for 5 mins before every session:

  • 1 min: C-curves
  • 1 min: S-curves
  • 1 min: Straight parallel lines
  • 2 mins: 10 perfect circles
